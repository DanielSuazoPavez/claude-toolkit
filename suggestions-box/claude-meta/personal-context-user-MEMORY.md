## 3. Board Games

### Taste Profile
Values design identity over production polish. Every game should have a reason to exist — whether that's a 4-hour logistics monster or a 15-minute pocket game. Zero patience for focus-grouped, bloated eurogames with nice components but no soul.

### Favorites & Preferences
- **Roads & Boats** — personal favorite. The logistics beast.
- **Splotter** — favorite publisher/designers. FCM, Antiquity, Bus, the lot. Wife's favorite game is FCM.
- **Oink Games** — second favorite publisher. Picks them up by color and position on the shelf.
- **Shinzawa** — great designer. Maskmen is the favorite Shinzawa game. 9 Lives is favorite trick-taking game, period. LUZ is also a highlight.
- **Japanese small games** — big part of the collection. Quick, quirky, with a clear design hook.
- **Trick-taking** — enjoys the genre, including Brazilian designs (Malabares, Pipoca by Arthur Lacerda).
- **18xx** — heavy period during the pandemic, online discord sessions multiple times a week.
- **Hates Stonemaier** — "genius marketing, great components, overly hyped and sometimes soulless designs." Likes Euphoria though.

### Playing Habits
- Evolved from "living in the heavy part of the spectrum" to lots of small games with sprinkles of heavy ones.
- Volunteers to teach games at meetups.
- Tracks plays in BG Stats. 208 different games in 2025.
- Captain Flip: ~5k plays on BGA (was rank 1), ~64 physical. Built a magnetic custom edition from scratch — aliexpress magnets, high-res art from publisher's site, chipboard tiles, metal ruler board. Works tilted. A proper custom edition, not a janky prototype.

### The Collection
- Curated, not accumulated. Games get sold regularly. Actively buys multiple games a month.
- Small games are harder to cull (cheap, take no space). Big euros that don't land get moved out.
- New Mill Industries incoming haul: Crisps, Agency, Tezuma Trick, Greasy Spoon, Reapers, Woodlanders.
- FLGS downtown handles Japanese imports, keeps prices around $30.
